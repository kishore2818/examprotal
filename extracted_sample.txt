
Unit 1: Electrostatics - Fundamental Principles and History

1. According to the text, which of the following forces experienced in everyday life is NOT electromagnetic in nature?

A. Tension in a string
B. Normal force from a surface
C. Gravitational force
D. Static friction

Answer: C
EXPLANATION: The text states that except gravity, all forces experienced in everyday life, such as tension, friction, and normal force, arise from electromagnetic interactions between atoms.
2. What is the microscopic origin of the ‘normal force’ that allows a person to stand on the Earth’s surface?
A. Strong nuclear interactions
B. The intrinsic mass of the person’s feet
C. Gravitational repulsion from the Earth’s core
D. Electromagnetic interaction of atoms at the contact surfaces
Answer: D
EXPLANATION: The normal force arises due to the electromagnetic interaction of atoms on the surface of the Earth with the atoms in the person’s feet.
3. Which material was first noticed by the Greeks two millenniums ago to attract small pieces of leaves and dust after being rubbed?

A. Amber
B. Rubber rod
C. Glass rod
D. Silk cloth

Answer: A
EXPLANATION: Greeks noticed that amber, a translucent fossilized resin, attracted light objects after being rubbed with fur.

6. What is the result when a charged rubber rod is brought near another charged rubber rod?

A. They attract each other
B. Nothing happens
C. They become electrically neutral
D. They repel each other

Answer: D
EXPLANATION: Like charges repel; since both are rubber rods charged in the same way, they possess like charges and repel each other.
7. The process of charging an object through rubbing is scientifically referred to as:

A. Electrostatic induction
B. Thermonic emission
C. Triboelectric charging
D. Electrolytic dissociation

Answer: C
EXPLANATION: Charging objects through friction or rubbing is known as triboelectric charging.
8. Which subatomic particle is described as having zero charge?

A. Proton
B. Electron
C. Neutron
D. Positron

Answer: C
EXPLANATION: Atoms are made of negatively charged electrons, positively charged protons, and neutrons which have zero charge.
9. If the net charge of an object is zero, the object is said to be:

A. Insulated
B. Electrically neutral
C. Negatively charged
D. Ionized

Answer: B
EXPLANATION: When the sum of positive and negative charges in an object results in zero net charge, it is electrically neutral.
10. What is the SI unit of electric charge?

A. Ampere
B. Volt
C. Coulomb
D. Ohm

Answer: C
EXPLANATION: The SI unit of charge is the coulomb (C).
11. Which fundamental property of particles is mentioned alongside mass as being intrinsic to the building blocks of matter?

A. Velocity
B. Electric charge
C. Volume
D. Temperature

Answer: B
EXPLANATION: The text states that electric charge is an intrinsic and fundamental property of particles, similar to mass.
12. When a glass rod is rubbed with silk, why does the silk become negatively charged?
A. Protons are transferred from the silk to the glass
B. Electrons are created on the silk by friction
C. Neutrons are converted into electrons on the silk surface
D. Negative charges are transferred from the glass to the silk
Answer: D
EXPLANATION: Rubbing simply transfers charges; in this case, some negative charges are transferred from the glass rod to the silk cloth.
13. Which statement best describes the Law of Conservation of Charges?
A. The total charge in any system always increases over time
B. The total electric charge in the universe is constant
C. Charge can be created but never destroyed
D. Charge can only be destroyed in a vacuum
Answer: B
EXPLANATION: The law states that total electric charge in the universe is constant and can neither be created nor destroyed, only transferred.
14. The interaction between the atoms in our hand and the atoms in an object when we push it is primarily:

A. Gravitational
B. Nuclear
C. Electromagnetic
D. Kinetic

Answer: C
EXPLANATION: The interaction between atoms during physical contact, like pushing an object, is electromagnetic in nature.
15. Electrostatics is the branch of electricity that deals with:

A. Charges in motion
B. Magnetic fields only
C. Stationary charges
D. Chemical energy

Answer: C
EXPLANATION: Electrostatics specifically deals with the behavior and related phenomena of charges at rest (stationary charges).
16. What did Benjamin Franklin conclude about the nature of rubbing two objects together?
A. Friction creates new electric energy from heat
B. Rubbing simply transfers charges from one object to another
C. Rubbing removes all charges from both objects
D. Rubbing converts protons into electrons
Answer: B
EXPLANATION: Franklin argued that rubbing doesn’t create charge but merely transfers it, maintaining the neutral balance if both are considered together.

25. Which of the following observations led to the inference that ‘like charges repel’?
A. A charged glass rod attracting paper
B. A charged rubber rod attracting a glass rod
C. Two charged rubber rods pushing away from each other

Answer: C
EXPLANATION: When two rods of the same material (e.g., rubber) are charged similarly and brought together, they repel, proving like charges repel.

C. Remains constant
D. Is always zero
Answer: C
EXPLANATION: Conservation of charge means that the total charge remains constant in any physical process; it is merely redistributed.
